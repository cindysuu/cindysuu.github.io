Life is crazy

I'm at a point in life where it feels like there's so many important moments that have happened already, but there's even more to come.

It's interesting to take a look back to the things that I have done and how they have shaped me as a person.

Some random but defining moments:

1. Going to China every summer. I haven't been in 5 years now, but before that for also about 5 years I was consistently traveling back to China and getting to explore the cities there. As a kid anything you do for such a long period of time feels normal because you don't have as much life experience, but looking back its a unique experience that really got me to love travel and being exposed to new things.

2. Attending regular coding classes in high school. I didn't realize my mom's full intentions at the time, and I wasn't particularly drawn to coding, but I think that being exposed early on to the concept of coding was really helpful.

3. Random phases of being into things. Art history, design, running, reading books, food and nutrition, etc. have made me a bit knowledgable in some areas and shed some light on who I am.

4. Working at Shake Shack for a summer. Feels kind of unreal but I was building burgers exactly like in the cooking games. I probably could have done something else but this was the summer after senior year where I wasn't really expected to do anything, and for some reason I wanted to do this.

5. Going to college. I think this is a big one for everyone. For me, I didn't have the insight that going out of state would be nice, but I'm glad that somehow I found myself on the opposite coast of the US. There's just so many things that I wouldn't have learned or experienced if I had stayed in a similar bubble to where I grew up in.
